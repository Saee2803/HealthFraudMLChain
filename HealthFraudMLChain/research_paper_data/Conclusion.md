# VII. CONCLUSION

The integration of artificial intelligence and blockchain technology in health insurance fraud detection has become essential for reducing financial losses, improving claim verification accuracy, and maintaining regulatory compliance in competitive healthcare environments. Existing fraud detection platforms primarily rely on static rule-based systems, manual investigations, and limited pattern recognition mechanisms, which often fail to detect sophisticated fraud schemes or provide real-time prevention. Many systems lack blockchain immutability, multi-stakeholder verification, structured security layers, and provider-specific collusion detection, thereby limiting their effectiveness in bridging the gap between traditional fraud prevention and modern security expectations. To overcome these limitations, the proposed HealthFraudMLChain system implements an AI-driven fraud detection platform featuring machine learning-based fraud scoring, blockchain-based immutable record-keeping, multi-layer security architecture, and role-based access control modules. The system also incorporates digital signatures for non-repudiation, ECIES encryption for sensitive data protection, collusion detection algorithms, and explainable AI features to enhance transparency and continuous fraud prevention.

Experimental usage demonstrated noticeable improvement in fraud detection accuracy, claim processing efficiency, and overall security posture after implementing the five-layer architecture. With its ability to provide automated fraud scoring, real-time notifications, and comprehensive audit trails, the system effectively enhances claim verification processes and supports investigators in identifying fraudulent patterns and tracking suspicious activities. Overall, this project demonstrates that AI-powered fraud detection systems integrated with blockchain technology can significantly improve insurance claim integrity and make quality fraud prevention accessible to healthcare providers regardless of organizational size or resources. Future enhancements may include advanced behavioral analysis, real-time anomaly detection using deep learning, cross-institutional fraud pattern sharing, and integration with national healthcare databases to further improve system effectiveness.
