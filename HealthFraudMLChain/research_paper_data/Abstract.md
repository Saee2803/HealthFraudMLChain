# Health Insurance Fraud Detection Using Artificial Intelligence and Blockchain Technology

---

## Abstract

**Abstract—** The increasing prevalence of fraudulent claims in the healthcare insurance sector has resulted in significant financial losses for insurers and higher premiums for policyholders. Traditional fraud detection methods rely heavily on manual verification and rule-based systems, which are time-consuming, error-prone, and incapable of identifying sophisticated fraud patterns. To address these challenges, this project presents an integrated platform that combines Machine Learning and Blockchain technology for automated health insurance fraud detection. The system analyzes insurance claim data to extract key features such as claim amount, diagnosis codes, treatment patterns, and provider information, and applies a trained Random Forest classifier with TF-IDF feature extraction to calculate fraud probability scores. Claims are processed through a multi-layer security architecture that includes role-based access control, smart-contract-like business rules, digital signatures using ECDSA for non-repudiation, and ECIES asymmetric encryption for protecting sensitive patient data. The platform also incorporates collusion detection algorithms to identify suspicious patterns between doctors and hospitals, along with insider threat monitoring to detect anomalous administrative behavior. Approved claims are immutably recorded on a blockchain ledger, ensuring transparency, auditability, and tamper-proof record keeping. The system provides a role-based interface for patients, doctors, and administrators, enabling efficient claim submission, review, and approval workflows with real-time notifications. Explainable AI (XAI) features offer interpretable fraud predictions, supporting informed decision-making by stakeholders. Functional evaluation and testing demonstrate that the system effectively reduces fraud detection time, improves accuracy over traditional methods, and maintains regulatory compliance through comprehensive audit trails, making it beneficial for healthcare providers, insurance companies, and regulatory bodies seeking a secure, data-driven approach to combating insurance fraud.

**Keywords—** *Health Insurance Fraud Detection, Machine Learning, Blockchain Technology, Random Forest Classifier, Digital Signatures, Smart Contracts, Role-Based Access Control, Explainable AI, Collusion Detection, Healthcare Security.*
