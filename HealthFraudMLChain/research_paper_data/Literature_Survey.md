# II. LITERATURE SURVEY

Artificial intelligence-based healthcare fraud detection systems have significantly advanced claim verification and fraud prevention, emphasizing accurate pattern recognition and personalized risk assessment mechanisms.[1] The integration of real-time claim analysis and anomaly detection using machine learning models has enhanced fraud detection platforms, improving detection accuracy and investigator efficiency.[2] Systems designed for insurance claim verification utilize AI-powered analytics for improved fraud identification and adaptive risk scoring.[3] Candidate assessment using AI-equipped platforms with blockchain integration capabilities has improved fraud prevention outcomes, aiding claim verification and trust building.[4] Additionally, extracting comprehensive claim profiles through pattern recognition and NLP techniques enhances fraud detection quality and personalization.[5] Finally, platforms integrated with blockchain technology assist in immutable record-keeping, improving overall reliability in claim verification and audit trail maintenance.[6] These advancements collectively strengthen AI applications in healthcare fraud detection, claim processing, and risk assessment.

**"Machine Learning Approaches for Health Insurance Fraud Detection"** R. Bauder, T. Khoshgoftaar (2024) [1]: This paper presents a machine learning-based fraud detection system that focuses on analyzing claim patterns to identify fraudulent activities. The system enhances detection accuracy by providing automated classification based on historical claim data and statistical analysis. It helps investigators gain awareness of potential fraud indicators and suspicious patterns. However, the system is primarily limited to pattern detection and does not include blockchain immutability or multi-stakeholder verification processes. The proposed HealthFraudMLChain system addresses these limitations by integrating machine learning detection with blockchain-based immutable record-keeping, digital signatures for non-repudiation, and role-based access control aligned with regulatory compliance requirements.

**"A Comprehensive Study and Implementation of Blockchain-Based Healthcare Data Management"** S. Tanwar, K. Parekh, R. Evans (2024) [2]: This paper presents a blockchain-based healthcare data management system that combines distributed ledger technology with cryptographic security to evaluate data integrity aspects during claim processing. The system focuses on immutability analysis and transaction verification to enhance data protection and audit capabilities. By incorporating blockchain-based interaction, the platform attempts to provide detailed tamper-proof records to stakeholders. However, the system requires high computational resources and lacks integration with machine learning for fraud detection on standard healthcare platforms, which limits its scalability. In contrast, the proposed HealthFraudMLChain system emphasizes web-based accessibility and scalability by providing AI-powered fraud detection, blockchain immutability, and multi-layer security modules that can be accessed through standard web browsers without the need for specialized infrastructure.

**"AI-Driven Healthcare Fraud Detection and Prevention"** M. Johnson, A. Williams (2024) [3]: In this research work, the authors utilize Natural Language Processing techniques to assess claim descriptions, medical codes, and structure of diagnosis information in healthcare claims. The system generates adaptive risk scores and provides feedback aimed at improving fraud identification and claim verification confidence. While the approach enhances detection accuracy and adaptability, the effectiveness of risk evaluation largely depends on data quality and predefined fraud pattern models. The proposed HealthFraudMLChain system addresses this limitation by combining fraud detection with interactive multi-role verification activities, smart-contract-like business rules, and structured approval mechanisms, thereby supporting overall claim verification beyond response pattern analysis.

**"A Survey of Blockchain Applications in Healthcare Insurance using Smart Contracts"** J. Chen, P. Kumar, O. Martinez (2024) [4]: This survey analyzes various blockchain-based healthcare insurance systems that employ smart contracts and distributed ledger techniques for automated claim processing and verification. The study highlights the scalability and adaptability of smart contract implementations in delivering personalized claim processing experiences. However, the surveyed systems often involve high computational costs and complex infrastructure requirements, which limit their practical deployment in healthcare environments. The HealthFraudMLChain system overcomes these challenges by utilizing efficient blockchain services and web-based technologies to deliver scalable fraud detection, claim verification, and approval process features without heavy infrastructure dependency.

**"An AI-Based Platform for Healthcare Insurance Claim Verification and Fraud Analysis"** Y. Zhang, F. Liu (2023) [5]: This study introduces an AI-based claim verification platform designed to simulate real-time fraud detection scenarios and provide automated risk evaluation. The system applies machine learning techniques to deliver instant fraud probability scores, improving investigator engagement and claim assessment. Although effective in fraud detection, the platform offers limited holistic security, as it does not integrate blockchain immutability, digital signatures, or structured multi-layer encryption. The proposed HealthFraudMLChain system addresses these limitations by offering a comprehensive fraud detection environment that includes blockchain-based immutable ledger, ECDSA digital signatures for non-repudiation, ECIES asymmetric encryption for sensitive data protection, and overall five-layer security architecture to support complete claim verification and regulatory compliance.

**"Ensemble Machine Learning Methods for Insurance Fraud Detection"** A. Sharma, R. Patel (2023) [6]: This research presents an ensemble-based machine learning approach combining Random Forest, XGBoost, and neural networks for insurance fraud detection. The system achieves high accuracy through feature engineering and model stacking techniques. However, it lacks explainability features and does not provide transparency in decision-making, which is crucial for regulatory compliance. The proposed HealthFraudMLChain system incorporates Explainable AI (XAI) features that provide interpretable fraud predictions, enabling stakeholders to understand the reasoning behind fraud scores and supporting informed decision-making in claim approval processes.

**"Collusion Detection in Healthcare Insurance using Network Analysis"** K. Brown, M. Davis (2023) [7]: This paper focuses on detecting collusion patterns between healthcare providers using graph-based network analysis and anomaly detection algorithms. The system identifies suspicious relationships and billing patterns among doctors, hospitals, and patients. While effective for network-based fraud detection, the system operates in isolation and does not integrate with claim processing workflows or provide real-time alerts. The HealthFraudMLChain system addresses this gap by incorporating collusion detection algorithms within the claim verification workflow, providing real-time notifications to administrators, and maintaining insider threat monitoring to detect anomalous behavior patterns across all system users.
