# Chapter 7: Problem Definition

---

The existing health insurance fraud detection systems rely heavily on manual verification and rule-based approaches which are time-consuming, error-prone, and incapable of identifying sophisticated fraud patterns. Traditional databases are vulnerable to tampering and do not provide immutable audit trails required for regulatory compliance. Current platforms lack multi-layer security architecture including digital signatures for non-repudiation and encryption for sensitive patient data protection. Most fraud detection systems analyze claims in isolation without detecting collusion patterns between healthcare providers. Machine learning-based systems operate as black boxes without providing explainable predictions to stakeholders. There is no unified platform that integrates automated fraud detection with blockchain-based immutable record-keeping and role-based access control for patients, doctors, and administrators.

The proposed system aims to develop an integrated web-based platform that combines Random Forest classifier with TF-IDF feature extraction for automated fraud probability scoring and blockchain technology for tamper-proof storage of approved claims. The system implements a five-layer security architecture including role-based access control, smart-contract-like business rules, ECDSA digital signatures, ECIES asymmetric encryption, and blockchain integrity verification. Additionally, it incorporates collusion detection algorithms, insider threat monitoring, explainable AI features, and real-time notification mechanisms to provide a comprehensive solution for health insurance fraud detection and prevention.
